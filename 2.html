Ziggurat

The Ziggurat of Ur is located in Ur, Mesopotamia, which is currently known as Iraq. It was built in the 21st century BC known as the bronze age. The Sumerians built it by covering both the interior and exterior in mud bricks and baked bricks. They gave the structure a base of 64 meters by 46 meters. The ziggurat was used for rituals like animal sacrifices for a patron god, storage of woven fabrics, grains, and gems as offerings to their god, storage of written down history and documents such as tax papers, managing irrigation systems, a center of trade, and an armory. Priests would perform rituals and manage the irrigation ditches that ran through the ziggurat. Out of all these, the structure's most prominent purpose was for the user(s) to be able to connect with their god. Some of the ziggurat remains and is open for tourists to visit.
