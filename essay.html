How did Architecture affect early civilizations?
The famous dictionary Oxford Languages states that architecture is “The art or practice of designing and constructing buildings” (Oxford Languages). The beginning of architecture can be traced back to the Neolithic period, in approximately 10,000 BC when people started living in homes. Comparing buildings built in different regions of the world and at different eras of history can give a person looking at the structure a better understanding of how people in different times lived. Architecture made it so early civilizations like Ancient Greece, Egypt, Ancient Rome, Ancient China, and Mesopotamia could easily respect their god or gods in religion, have entertainment, and protect themselves from enemies. Three elaborate structures, known as the Temple of Solomon, the Ziggurat, and the Parthenon participated in aiding people’s worship of their god or gods and in some cases glorifying their city. The Temple of Solomon was constructed because King Solomon had a vision where he met God and was told to build the temple. Solomon believed that if citizens had a connection to God they would bond through religion. This shows that it was a place where civilians could praise their god. Additionally at the peak of the ziggurat of Ur, usually priests would carry out ceremonies containing sacrifice for the patron god. Within the Ziggurat they would store grains, woven fabrics, and gems as contributions to their god or gods. The Parthenon helped people pray to the Goddess Athena, the patron goddess of Athens, and to whom the Parthenon’s construction was dedicated. Most days people would travel to the Parthenon just to worship her. This is pivotal because it indicates that the Parthenon also helped connect people through faith. Not only did the Parthenon help connect people in Athens but it also is believed to have the golden ratio which helped glorify the empire. Three intricate structures took place in helping people respect their god or gods and enhance their city. While the Colosseum provided leisure and amusement for the ancient city of Rome, the Great Wall of China provided a source of protection for its people from northern invaders. The emperor paid for all expenditures in the Colosseum in order to build up his reputation and support. The Colosseum was the Roman citizens’ main source of entertainment. This is because it was the largest amphitheater in Rome and could hold approximately 50,000 people. If you were a spectator inside the Colosseum, you would witness either gladiator fights, animal hunts, or mock naval battles. Furthermore, the Great Wall of China protected the civilians of the Qin Dynasty from attackers in the north, such as nomads from inner Asia. Attributes such as its humongous length spanning around 13,170.7 miles or 21,196.18 kilometers long, and its access to quickly inform other watch towers about invaders through the use of smoke, fire, and quick and easy transportation along the border helped it become such a reliable source of security for its people. The Great Wall of China offered a source of safety for its people from northern intruders, during which the Colosseum granted relaxation and entertainment for the early city of Rome. If everything is taken into account, Architecture made it a possibility for early civilizations like Ancient Greece, Egypt, Ancient Rome, Ancient China, and Mesopotamia to effortlessly respect their god or gods in their faith, have easy access to entertainment, and guard themselves against enemies.
