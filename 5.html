Parthenon

Built between 447 BC through 432 BC, the Parthenon is an ancient Greek structure located in Athens, Greece, on the Acropolis. Architects, Ictinus and Callicrates, under the wardship of Phidias, a sculptor, undertook the task of building the magnificent structure after orders from Pericles. Pericles was an imminent Greek general, rhetor, statesman, and tyrant. He wanted the Parthenon built to help revamp Athens because they had a Golden Age. He also wanted to strengthen their leadership in the Delian league. Its construction was dedicated to the Greek goddess Athena. For this reason, there was a gold and ivory statue of her located inside it. However, it is no longer there. It is unclear what happened to it, but it is believed to be looted. Ictinus, Callicrates, and Phidias primarily used marble to construct the Parthenon.
