Colosseum

The Colosseum is an ancient Roman structure located east of the Palatine Hill in Rome or current day Italy. It was used for entertainment, such as gladiator fights where slaves would usually battle to their death, animal hunts, and mock naval battles because it could fit around fifty-thousand spectators. Spectators did have to pay to watch, though. Its completion occurred in AD eighty-two and was built by enslaved Jews from Judaea. The materials they used consisted of travertine, Tuff, concrete, bricks, marble, lime, mortar, and stone. They also used complex systems such as barrel vaults and groin vaults for the structure. The Colosseum was paid for by the government and still stands today with around an annual seven million visitors.
