Temple of Solomon

The Temple of Solomon, located in the center of Jerusalem, was ordered to be built by King Solomon, the third king of Israel. He obtained funds for the temple by taxing his citizens large sums of money. Due to this, his people grew a dislike for him. The Tyrians, or the primary builders of the temple, built the temple's interior out of cedar wood covered in gold. The exterior contained two hollow pillars made of bronze called “Boaz” and “Jachin”. The main doors of the temple were made of olive wood overlaid in gold. The Temple of Solomon was used for religious activities such as prayer and praise; it held the Ark of the Covenant, which comprised the tablets of Moses, and was meant to bring people together through said religious activities. Ultimately the Temple of Solomon led to Solomon’s demise and is believed to be destroyed in 586 or 587 BC.
