Great Wall of China

Surrounding some of northern China's border, the Great Wall of China is around 13,170.7 miles or 21, 196.18 kilometers long. The Great Wall of China was constructed over 2,500 years, typically by poor peasants. They were originally ordered by Shi Huangdi, the first Emperor of the Qin dynasty, who sought to unify China during the warring states period. Shi Huangdi set principles for weights and measurements, laws, and currency, and constructed a 4,000-mile highway system. Even though he did this, his people were enraged under his rule. Scholars hated him because he burned books, and workers had a strong dislike for him because he forced them to work on the wall without pay and were forced to either work or die. Most of the workers ended up dead and were buried along the base of it. As a result of the society’s discontent, shortly after Shi Huangdi's death, the Qin Dynasty fell around 202 BC. Workers would predominantly use stone, brick, lime, and wood while building it, though this did vary depending on the resources at hand. The main points of this structure were to help communicate and to defend China’s northern border from invaders such as the Huns and Mongolians; nevertheless, it is now a tourist attraction.
